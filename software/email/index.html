Email Clients

Discover free, open-source, and secure email clients, along with some email alternatives you may not have considered.

Email Warning

When using end-to-end encryption (E2EE) technology like OpenPGP, the email will still have some metadata that is not encrypted in the header of the email. Read more about email metadata.

OpenPGP also does not support Forward secrecy, which means if either your or the recipient's private key is ever stolen, all previous messages encrypted with can be exposed/visible in plaintext. How do I protect my private keys?

Rather than use email for prolonged conversations & communications, consider using a medium that does support Forward secrecy. It's best to be as secure as possible.

Recommended Instant Messengers

Desktop Email Clients

Thunderbird

Thunderbird is a free, open source, cross-platform email, newsgroup, news feed, and chat (XMPP, IRC, Twitter) client developed by the Thunderbird community, and previously by the Mozilla Foundation.

Website Privacy Policy

Mailpile

Mailpile is a free, community funded, open source email client with user-friendly encryption and privacy features.

Website Privacy Policy

Mailvelope

Mailvelope is a browser extension that enables the exchange of encrypted emails following the OpenPGP encryption standard.

Website Privacy Policy

Worth Mentioning

  • NeoMutt - NeoMutt is an open-source command line mail reader (or MUA) for Linux and BSD. It’s a fork of Mutt with added features.
  • Rainloop - RainLoop is a web based email client. Simple, modern & fast web-based email client.

Mobile Email Clients

Android Email Clients

FairEmail

FairEmail is a minimal, open source email app, using open standards (IMAP, SMTP, OpenPGP) with a low data and battery usage.

K-9 Mail

K-9 Mail is an independent mail application that supports both POP3 and IMAP mailboxes, but only supports push mail for IMAP.
